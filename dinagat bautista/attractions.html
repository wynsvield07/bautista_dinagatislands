Tourist Attractions

Mount Redondo Natural Bonsai Forest

The Mount Redondo Natural Bonsai Forest in Loreto, Dinagat Islands, is a unique 100-hectare forest featuring naturally stunted trees averaging only two feet tall. Species such as narra, molave, and ironwood grow in miniature form due to the island’s mineral-rich soil, creating a rare ecological wonder.

Blue Lagoon

The Blue Lagoon, also known as the Pangabangan Tidal Pool, is a hidden natural pool in Libjo, Dinagat Islands. Surrounded by limestone cliffs and white sand, the lagoon’s clear turquoise water makes it a perfect place for swimming and relaxation.

Campintac Black Beach No. 2

Campintac Black Beach No. 2 in Loreto is a historically important site where the 6th U.S. Army Ranger Battalion landed in 1944 before the Battle of Leyte. Today it is a peaceful beach destination with a memorial honoring the liberation forces.
